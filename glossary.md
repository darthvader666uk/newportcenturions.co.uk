    <h1 itemprop="headline">Korfball Glossary</h1>
    <p class="intro">New to korfball? Use this glossary to understand key terms and concepts used in the sport. Whether you're learning the basics or deepening your knowledge, these definitions will help you master korfball terminology.</p>

    <div class="glossary-container">

      <section class="glossary-section">
        <h2>Core Korfball Concepts</h2>
        
        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Korfball</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">A fast-paced, mixed-gender team sport with similarities to netball and basketball. Teams have 8 players on court: 4 male and 4 female, each defending their own gender. No dribbling is allowed.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>See also:</strong> <a href="#mixed-gender">Mixed-Gender Sport</a>, <a href="#no-dribbling">No Dribbling Rule</a>, <a href="#basketball-netball">Comparison to netball and basketball</a></p>
        </div>

        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Mixed-Gender Sport</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">A sport where teams must include equal numbers of male and female players, ensuring both genders play simultaneously and with equal importance to the team.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>Unique to korfball:</strong> True gender equality in competitive sports</p>
        </div>

        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Korf</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">The basket or hoop used in korfball, positioned 3.5 meters (11 feet 6 inches) high. Teams have a korf at each end of the court.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>Height:</strong> 3.5 meters (11'6") - same for all players</p>
        </div>
      </section>

      <section class="glossary-section">
        <h2>Rules & Gameplay</h2>
        
        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term" id="no-dribbling">No Dribbling Rule</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">In korfball, players cannot bounce the ball while moving. Once you receive the ball, you must pass it or shoot; you cannot move while holding it.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>Impact:</strong> Emphasizes teamwork and accurate passing</p>
        </div>

        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Gender-Specific Defending</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">A korfball rule where male players can only defend other male players, and female players can only defend female players. This ensures equal competition between genders.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>Result:</strong> Every player has equal defensive responsibility</p>
        </div>

        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Basket</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">The goal in korfball. Throwing the ball through the korf (basket) at either end of the court. Each basket scores 1 point.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>Scoring:</strong> 1 point per basket regardless of distance</p>
        </div>

        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Free Pass</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">An uncontested pass awarded after a rule violation or stoppage in play, allowing a team to resume play safely.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>Used for:</strong> Restarting play fairly</p>
        </div>
      </section>

      <section class="glossary-section">
        <h2>Court & Positions</h2>
        
        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Court</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">The rectangular playing area in korfball, approximately 40 meters long and 20 meters wide, divided into two halves with a korf at each end.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>Dimensions:</strong> 40m × 20m (standard)</p>
        </div>

        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Attacking Zone</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">The half of the court where a team is trying to score. In korfball, teams switch ends halfway through each half to ensure fairness.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>Fair play:</strong> Teams take turns on both ends</p>
        </div>

        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Defending Zone</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">The half of the court a team is defending. Players try to prevent the opposing team from scoring baskets at their end.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>Connected to:</strong> <a href="#gender-defending">Gender-Specific Defending</a></p>
        </div>
      </section>

      <section class="glossary-section">
        <h2>Club & Competitive Terms</h2>
        
        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Welsh League</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">The primary competitive korfball league in Wales, organized by the Welsh Korfball Association. Newport Centurions are Welsh League Champions.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>Newport record:</strong> Champions 2022/2023 & 2025</p>
        </div>

        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Teamwork</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">The core principle of korfball. With no dribbling allowed, successful play depends entirely on accurate passing and coordinated team movement.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>What it means:</strong> No single player can dominate; everyone contributes</p>
        </div>

        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Coaching</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">Professional guidance provided by experienced korfball coaches. Newport Centurions coaches help players of all levels improve their skills and understanding of the game.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>Available to:</strong> All training levels from beginner to advanced</p>
        </div>

        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Training Session</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">A scheduled time for team members to practice korfball skills, improve fitness, and work on team tactics. Newport Centurions holds training on Tuesdays and Thursdays.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>Newport times:</strong> Tuesdays 6-8 PM, Thursdays 8-9 PM</p>
        </div>

        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Beginner-Friendly</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">A sport or program designed to welcome people with no prior experience. Korfball is beginner-friendly because the rules are straightforward and teams develop players through practice.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>For you:</strong> <a href="/join-us/">Join Newport Centurions as a beginner</a></p>
        </div>

        <div class="glossary-item" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Definition">
          <h3 itemprop="term">Substitution</h3>
          <p itemprop="description">Replacing one player with another during a korfball match. Teams can make substitutions to manage player fatigue or adjust tactics.</p>
          <p class="definition-note"><strong>Use in matches:</strong> All teams use subs to stay fresh</p>
        </div>
